GobiScholars Internship Program

The Gobischolars Summer Internship Program is a two-month opportunity designed for U.S. college students passionate about teaching their favorite subject(s) to Mongolian high school students. Interns will collaborate with a dynamic entrepreneurial team, participate in hands-on projects, and receive guidance and networking opportunities tailored to their areas of interest.

This program blends professional development, cultural immersion, and adventure, offering exclusive workshops, career-building activities, and unique cultural experiences. Whether it’s exploring Mongolia’s heritage, teaching impactful lessons, or engaging with local communities, participants will contribute meaningfully while gaining valuable skills for their futures.
Program Dates:

  • Application Deadline: February 10, 2025
  • Arrival in Ulaanbaatar: June 10, 2025
  • Program Ends: August 15, 2025
  • Departure: August 16–23, 2025

This program is open to U.S. undergraduate students (sophomores, juniors, and seniors) with a minimum GPA of 3.5, a strong commitment to education, and relevant academic expertise in their chosen teaching subject.

For more information or to apply, contact info@gobischolars.com

Eligibility Criteria

We welcome applications from U.S. university students who meet the following requirements.

➤

Enrollment Status

Must be a current undergraduate student (sophomore, junior, or senior) at a U.S. university.

➤

Academic Excellence

GPA of 3.5 or higher (on a 4.0 scale).

➤

Commitment to Education

Demonstrated enthusiasm for teaching and inspiring younger generations.

➤

Financial Independence

Must be able to sponsor your participation in the program (as this is an unpaid internship with an NGO).

➤

Relevant Academic Background

Must be majoring in the subject they intend to teach during the internship (minors or unrelated fields will not be considered).

Below are common courses we are looking for, but not limited to: Computer Science and Technology, Business, Engineering, Environmental Studies, Political Science, Education, Communications and Media Studies, Anthropology.

If you meet these criteria and are eager to contribute your expertise while immersing yourself in a transformative cultural experience, we encourage you to apply!

Required Application Materials

Applicants for the Mongolia Summer Internship Program must submit the following materials to complete their application.

➤

1. Application Form

Complete the official application form with accurate and up-to-date information that contains all required materials.

➤

2. Identification Documents

Provide copies of your passport or government-issued ID card.

➤

3. Resume

Submit your current resume that highlights your academic achievements, professional experience, and extracurricular activities.

➤

4. Motivational Letter

Write a motivation letter including:

  • Indicate the subject you are interested in teaching.
  • Explain your reasons for choosing this subject, including how your academic background, experiences, and interests align with it and how you hope to inspire others through teaching it.
➤

5. Lesson Plan

Provide a concise lesson plan outlining the subject you intend to teach, including key objectives, activities, and methods to engage students effectively.

➤

6. Letters of Recommendation

Submit 1–2 letters of recommendation from professors, mentors, or supervisors who can attest to your:

  • Academic abilities.
  • Personal character.
  • Suitability for this program.
➤

7. Proof of Academic Standing

Include a copy of your most recent official transcript to verify your academic performance.

➤

8. Optional Materials

Applicants may choose to submit additional materials that showcase their qualifications, such as:

  • A portfolio or samples of work (for fields like art, design, or writing).
  • Certificates, awards, or other recognitions relevant to the internship.
➤

Submission Instructions

All required materials must be submitted via Application Form.

Legal Consultation

U.S. citizens can visit Mongolia without a visa for up to 90 days for tourism or short-term visits. Currently, there are no specific legal provisions for international students in Mongolia. If any legal issues arise, interns are advised to contact the U.S. Embassy for assistance.

Insurance Guidelines

Insurance coverage for incoming interns will depend on their individual plans. Most U.S.-based insurance does not cover services abroad. Interns with international insurance should confirm that Mongolia is included in their coverage. For added security, purchasing insurance from a Mongolian provider for the duration of the two-month stay is recommended.
